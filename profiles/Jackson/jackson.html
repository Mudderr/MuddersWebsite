Pre Apocalypse

Jackson grew up in a toxic household in Dallas, Texas, with his mother Abigail and his father Robert. His father was an abusive alcoholic, and his mother, though kind-hearted, was too submissive and fearful to stand up to her husband. This created a volatile and unsafe environment for Jackson, who never quite fit in with the others in his neighborhood.

Shortly before the apocalypse began, a particularly violent altercation erupted between Jackson's parents. His father, in a drunken rage, began attacking his mother. Jackson, unable to watch his mother suffer any longer, intervened. The situation quickly escalated, and in a moment of self-defense, Jackson struck his father with a blunt object. The blow was fatal, and Robert died instantly.

Panicked and overwhelmed with guilt, Jackson fled the scene. For two days, he evaded the authorities, who eventually caught up with him. As they were escorting him to the police car, the apocalypse began. A group of infected attacked the officers, creating chaos and giving Jackson an opportunity to escape. He took advantage of the distraction and fled, avoiding the repercussions of his actions due to the sudden outbreak.

Start of apocalypse

When the zombie apocalypse hit, Jackson was just 17. Early on, he found himself teaming up with two other survivors: Luke, an older teen from his neighborhood, and Daniel, a young man with a sharp mind a nd a knack for strategic thinking.

In the early days of the apocalypse, Jackson, Mark, and Daniel were cornered by a horde of zombies. They made a desperate run for it, but Luke didnt make it, and Daniel was bitten on the arm. In a harrowing attempt to save him, Jackson had to cut off Daniels arm, hoping to stop the infection. Daniel ended up bleeding out and dying.

Mid Apocalypse

Jackson's motorcycle became his lifeline in the apocalyptic wasteland. It allowed him to cover vast distances quickly, outrun hordes of zombies, and carry essential supplies. His trusty machine gun became his weapon of choice, providing both protection and a means to clear paths through the undead.

During a confrontation with another survivor over scarce resources, Jackson was engaged in a brutal one-on-one fight. The other survivor slashed his right eye, leaving him blind on that side.

Despite his tough exterior, Jackson carries the emotional scars of his past and the apocalypse. He copes by adopting a laid-back and somewhat silly demeanor, often masking his pain with humor. He drinks and smokes heavily, using these vices as a way to numb his emotional wounds and deal with the harsh reality of his world
